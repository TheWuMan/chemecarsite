Jennifer Li

Jennifer is currently a senior studying Chemical Engineering. She joined Cornell ChemE Car in January 2013 and has been a member of the Biological Systems sub-team ever since. In addition to ChemE Car, Jennifer has completed a co-op at Johnson & Johnson and is an undergraduate research assistant in the DeLisa Research Group. In her free time, Jennifer is an avid Seahawks fan and serves as an intramural official for soccer, volleyball, and basketball.

  • Subteam: Biological Systems
  • Joined: Fall 2012

James Dong

James Dong grew up in Ridgefield, CT. Currently, he is a Junior, pursuing a B.S. in Chemical Engineering. James joined the Battery Sub-Team during fall semester of his freshman year and has been an active member since. During his freshman year, he proposed the aluminum-air battery. With the help of the entire team, this battery proceeded to win first place at Regionals 2014. James currently oversees Battery Sub-Team research and development and data analysis. In his free time, he enjoys traveling and playing guitar.

  • Subteam: Battery
  • Joined: Fall 2013

Ngan (Sophie) Le

Sophie is a junior in Chemical Engineering. She joined ChemE Car’s Safety in fall 2014 as a sophomore. Her research interest includes engineering safety, computational bioengineering and working in pilot plant. As Safety subteam leader, Sophie is responsible for ensuring laboratory standards and safe operation across the team and mentoring new Safety members. In fall 2015, she compiled the Engineering Documentation Package (EDP) for the Battery Car and collaborated with Cornell EHS in shipping hazardous chemicals to competition site in Salt Lake City, Utah. On campus, Sophie is actively involved with Cornell International Students Admissions Ambassadors (ISAA), and Cornell Vietnamese Association. Fun fact about Sophie: She can breathe through the corners of her eyes!

  • Subteam: Safety
  • Joined: Fall 2014
